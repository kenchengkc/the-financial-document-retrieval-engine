In its most recent Form 10-K, Apple identifies concentration of manufacturing and supply with a limited number of partners — many located outside the United States — as a principal supply-chain risk[1]. The company notes that single- or limited-source components, including custom silicon, expose it to shortages, price volatility, and quality issues that it may not be able to mitigate in the short term[2].

Management states that prolonged disruption could materially affect its ability to meet product demand and could adversely impact results of operations[4].
